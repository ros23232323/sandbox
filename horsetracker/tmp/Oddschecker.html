Sporting Life Preview

This won't take much winning but the race is likely to come soon enough for Baraymi, who was well beaten at Newbury on Saturday and has gone backwards since finishing second in a maiden hurdle at Wetherby in January. Polytrack winner BYRON FLYER has hinted at ability in both starts over timber, staying on late at Ludlow last month despite racing a little keenly. His stable is in good form and this looks an excellent opportunity. Ex-pointer Gold Mountain may be better than stablemate Tactical Manoeuvre but that's not saying much, though Bleu Et Noir should be a bigger threat if ready after a break.

Oddschecker Verdict

BLEU ET NOIR returns from a break with the Tim Vaughan yard in fine form and he had made an encouraging start to his hurdling career when fourth at Market Rasen on his penultimate start. His sole subsequent performance saw him fall at the fourth in a hot contest at Huntingdon, won by O O Seven, and he gets the nod here. Baraymi has to confirm the promise he showed when second at Wetherby in January, although he was not totally disgraced when midfield at Newbury on Saturday. Any market support for Rules newcomer Gold Mountain would have to be noted.

Prediction: 1st Bleu Et Noir, 2nd Baraymi, 3rd Gold Mountain
